About Me

I earned my PhD in loop quantum gravity with the highest honors from the University of the Basque Country in 2023. Shortly thereafter, I joined the Centre de Physique Th√©orique at the University of Aix-Marseille as a CNRS Postdoctoral Researcher. In 2025, I returned to my alma mater, where I am currently serving as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Physics.

My research focuses on black holes as windows to quantum gravity phenomena, particularly exploring how a discrete spacetime structure might address singularities. To this end, I develop effective models inspired by loop quantum gravity. My long-term objective is to deepen our understanding of quantum gravity and provide insights into the fundamental nature of spacetime.

To enhance collaborations, stay informed about the latest advancements in the field, and maximize the impact of my research through knowledge exchange and joint initiatives, I actively participate in the International Society on Loop Quantum Gravity (ISLQG), the Sociedad Española de Gravitación y Relatividad (SEGRE), and the EHU Quantum Center (EHU QC).

Research

I explore modified Hamiltonian models aimed at obtaining singularity-free spacetimes inspired by loop quantum gravity. My primary focus is to ensure explicit covariance in the resulting effective theory, enabling the prediction of measurable quantum-gravity effects while exploring and enjoying the novel geometries.
